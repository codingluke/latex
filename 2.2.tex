In der vorliegenden Bachelorarbeit werden %drei Verschaltungsvarianten von Collier, Lojewski und Angelino für

Der Wirkungsgrad dieser Kreisläufe ist im Vergleich zu anderen Stromerzeugungsprozessen mit durchschnittlich 31\% niedrig. \section*{Abstract}
In this thesis the design of a binary potassium-steam cycle is discussed and by simulation and analyse of exergy  compared. 
Steam processes are limited, because of the evaporation properties of the water. The loads on the material is high and thus the components of the cycle are limited in the optimisation. 
The efficiency of these cycles, compared to other processes for generating energy, are with 31\% low. As previous studies have shown, binary steam processes increases
the efficiency up to 50\%.\par
Material properties and studies on the suitability in a binary steam process of different alkali metals
and mercury are gathered. Potassium is found to be the most appropriate fluid
since it has a low vapor pressure at high temperatures.\par
Three interconection models are compared to each other. Based on the processes of
Collier, Lojewski and Angelino processes are simulated and exergy analysis are made.
The calculation of the compared interconection variants leads to statements regarding the exergy destruction of the components used and the studied interconnection.
It turns out, that the Angelino-process in which the waste heat of the potassium from the second turbine is used for the heating of the water cycle represents the most reasonable choice. This finding is based on energy and exergy analyse.\par
The work presents which influence the reheating and heat exchanger have as a link between the potassium and water cycle for the increase of efficiency in binary steam processes. The exergy analysis shows that in every process the potassium evaporator has the largest and the heat exchanger between the potassium and the water cycle has the next larger exergy destruction. This results in an improvement of the components.
%The subject of this thesis is the design of a binary potassium-steam cycle simulation, which allows to perform a exergetic analysis. 
%Based on the results, perhaps in the future a complete power plant process will be developed. The Review of the literature shows that the binary potassium-steam process due to the higher process temperature and the relatively small vapor pressure of potassium cycle can provide an interesting perspective for the future.\par

%Results of the exergetic analysis indicate the points at which the process has the greatest potential for improvement. The steam generator should be improved in terms of the minimum temperature difference. This would lower its destruction of exergy and raise the optimum of potassium condensing pressure. Also the low and the high pressure preheating in the water cycle could be improved. Furthermore, it would make sense to install a second reheater in the water cycle, making this more efficient and increasing the heat output of the potassium cycle. Thus, the potassium condensing pressure can be reduced without causing cavitation in the potassium pump.\par

%For the realized simple condensation stage of potassium, the results show that the exergetic efficiency of the overall process dependents considerably on potassium condensation pressure. In the range from 0.2 to 0.4\,bar the exergetic efficiency is increased with decreasing condensation pressure by more than 1.1 percentage points. The parameter analysis also shows that an optimum potassium condensation pressure exists below 0.2\, bar. To reduce the pressure, it must be ensured that no cavitation occurs in the potassium pump.
